Job Specification:
Desired Qualifications:
Like in every job, we ask for our applicant to have qualifications related to IT so we can find the most suitable applicant for the job role.
This can include:

·         GCSE in IT (or BTEC equivalent)

·         Level 2 award in creating a website.

·         Level 2 certificate in interactive media

·         Level 2/3 Diploma in creative media

·         Level 3 Diploma in information technology (specialist)

·         Level 3 Diploma in IT user skills

·         The candidate must also understand HTML, CSS, JavaScript as they will be mostly focused on throughout the job.

 What we expect from you:
We look for candidates with high ambition, talanted developer that will always have a positive attitude and have a solid baseline of technical skills as well as good communication skills and time managment skills.		 Key skills of an IT Developer:
  • Mathematical aptitude.
  • Problem-solving skills.
  • Programming languages (different types of developer roles require different technologies).
  • Excellent organisational and time management skills.
  • Accuracy and attention to detail.
  • An understanding of technology trends and their role in a commercial environment.
  • Teamworking skills.
  • Self-development skills to keep up to date with a fast-changing industry.
 Responsibilities:
As a web-designer, you will need to:

·         Performing and directing any website updates

·         Writing, designing and editing website content.

·         Designing user interfaces and navigation menus.

·         Writing and reviewing code for sites, typically HTML, XML, or Javascript.

·         Integrating multimedia content onto a site

·         Testing web applications.

·         Identity errors through testing or customer feedback and correcting any errors or referring them to relevant employees to be improved.

·         Fix bugs in existing projects or referring to a relevant employee to be fixed.

·         Meet designers, developers and project staff for progress updates.

·         Gather requirements from clients and users.

·         Stay up to date with new trends and advancements in web development.

·         Attend and speak at web development conferences and workshops.
